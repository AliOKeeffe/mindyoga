Yoga Poses for Your Mental Wellbeing

About Mind Yoga

Our mission at Mind yoga is to provide support and healing to those experiencing difficult emotions through teaching the art of yoga. It is our core belief that practicing yoga can improve a person's mental wellbeing and relax the mind.

Regular yoga practice creates calmness and mental clarity, increases body awareness and relieves stress patterns. Specific yoga poses can help release certain difficult emotions and at Mind Yoga we will guide you though these poses step by step so you can get the maximum benefits.

Yoga Poses

Anxiety - Upward Facing Dog Pose (Urdhva Mukha Svanasana)

Upward-Facing Dog pose opens your heart, helping you remove any pent-up emotions in your chest. This stretch from head to toe keeps a special focus on the spine. Stress and anxiety trigger cortisol release, which stimulates "trigger" points, often along our spine, in our parasympathetic and sympathetic nervous systems. By focusing on releasing these points through upward-facing dog pose and learning to be present, our stress response, cortisol, and adrenaline levels can become more balanced and, in turn, shift our brain chemistry.

  1. Lie on your stomach, with your hands next to the ribs, fingers pointing forwards, elbows tucked into your sides.
  2. Press the tops of your feet into the floor and engage the thighs and knees, and keep the tailbone pointing towards the heels.
  3. On an inhalation press into your hands and feet, straighten your arms and lift your chest and legs off the floor.
  4. Engage your lower belly and knit your lower ribs.
  5. Draw the shoulder blades onto your back and lift your breastbone.

Grief - Standing Forward Fold (Uttanasana)

Forward bending Yoga poses are soothing and nourishing. Forward Folds also help to calm you down because they encourage you to relax into the pose while pulling your senses inward, which allows you to rest for a few moments in your own inner essence. Forward Bending Yoga poses also allow you to curl your body into itself while protecting your heart and abdominal areas. Additionally, Forward Folds open up your hips and first chakra area, which releases stagnant survival energy and helps you to feel more empowered and grounded.

  1. At the back of the mat, step your feet hip-width distance apart and on an exhalation, fold over your legs.
  2. If you are able, rest the crown of your head on a block, which can be incredibly restorative. Otherwise, simply let your head hang.
  3. Your arms will dangle loosely, palms on the floor or clasp opposite elbows.
  4. Hold for 15 breaths.
  5. Round your way to standing, very slowly, one vertebra at a time. Let your head be the last thing to come up.

Trauma - Child's Pose (Balasana)

This is a heart opening pose when performed with an open mind, the full-body, gravitational pull induces a great sense of physical, mental, and emotional relief. It releases tension in the shoulders, neck, back, and hips too. This pose also helps to promote relaxation by encouraging steady conscious breathing, which is particularly great for trauma sufferers due to a calming of the sympathetic nervous system.

  1. Begin on your hands and knees with your big toes touching. Your hands should be directly under your arms, and your legs should be hip-distance apart.
  2. Exhale as you bow forward allowing your torso to rest on or between your thighs with your forehead on the mat. Keeping your arms long and extended, press into your hands keeping your sitz bones on your heels.
  3. Hold this pose for as long as you need to. When finished, slowly use your hands to walk your torso upright and sit back on your heels.

Stress - Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

Tree pose is fundamental in easing stress. By implementing basic standing balances, you promote concentration, focus, and awareness, with the intention of taking your mind away from stress and placing your attention on your physical self.

  1. Start by standing straight with a long, tall back and your feet aligned and touching. Your arms should be straight along either side of your body.
  2. Take a few breaths and find a place or object in the room to focus your attention. Slowly shift your weight to your left leg and begin to raise your right foot off the floor. Align the sole of your right foot with the inside of your left thigh. The toes should be pointing down and your pelvis should be completely straight.
  3. Stretch your arms straight up toward the ceiling with palms pressed together forming an inverted V.
